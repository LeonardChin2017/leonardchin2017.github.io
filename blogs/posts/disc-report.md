## About the Leading Self Program

The Leading Self Program (LSP) is a program provided by Fish Camp Learning and sponsored by Continental Automotive that aims to enhance the soft skills of its employees. In this program, DISC is used to assess personality.

## About DISC

The DISC profile traces its roots back to the early 1920s when it was introduced by psychologist William Moulton Marston. His model aimed to understand human behavior by identifying four primary types of behavioral expression based on a person's perception of themselves in relation to their environment.

Today, DISC serves as a comprehensive tool for comprehending behavior strengths and limitations, enhancing communication styles, and fostering effective teamwork.

The four styles:

- **Dominance (D)** — Goal-oriented go-getters. They thrive on accomplishing tasks and radiate confidence. Motto: *"Let's get things done!"*
- **Influence (I)** — Social butterflies who love to connect with and persuade others. Their enthusiasm is infectious.
- **Steadiness (S)** — Harmony-focused individuals who value stability and consistency. Always there to lend a hand.
- **Compliance (C)** — Meticulous perfectionists who set high standards and focus on accuracy. Quality is their top priority.

## My DISC Report

Based on the DISC report, I am a **Designer Style (C & D, predominantly C)**.

As a Designer, I am a highly task-oriented individual who is sensitive to problems. I am driven and have an effective problem-solving approach. My decision-making is based on facts rather than emotions, and I tend to be quiet and cautious in trusting others.

I excel in analytical thinking and value fact-based techniques. I reject quick fixes. Commitment is essential to me, and I expect others to uphold their commitments as well. Quality is of utmost importance — a job should be done right or not at all.

At times, I can become too absorbed in my work and struggle to delegate tasks. I have a strong desire to gather all the necessary facts before making decisions, which can be perceived as a delay by others.

### General Characteristics of Designer Style People

- Analytical
- High ego strength
- Problem solver
- Extremely task-oriented

### Motivated By

- Work and project completion
- Power and authority to design and implement solutions
- Not having to needlessly socialize or play politics
- Being provided the necessary tools to achieve success

### Ideal Environment

- Being able to design and refine
- Challenging tasks and activities
- Projects that produce tangible results
- Recognition for analytical abilities

## My Personal Growth Areas as CD Style

- Strive to be an "active" listener
- Be attentive to other team members' ideas until everyone reaches a consensus
- Be friendlier and more approachable
- Develop a greater appreciation for the opinions, feelings, and desires of others
- Put more energy into personal relationships and team support
